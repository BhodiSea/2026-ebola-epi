Ebola Bundibugyo Virus Disease Situation Report – DRC

Published: 2026-05-20

Africa CDC continues to monitor the ongoing Bundibugyo virus disease outbreak in Ituri Province, Democratic Republic of Congo. As of 20 May 2026, the Africa CDC Regional Integrated Surveillance and Laboratory Network (RISLNET) reports 47 confirmed cases and 12 deaths (CFR 25.5%) since the outbreak was declared on 20 April 2026.

The outbreak remains concentrated in Bunia health zone. Rapid response teams from Africa CDC have been deployed alongside WHO and MSF partners. Contact tracing is ongoing with 312 contacts currently under follow-up.

Africa CDC is coordinating with the Member State to ensure adequate PPE supply and safe burial teams are operational across all affected health zones in Ituri Province.

The next situation report will be issued on 27 May 2026.
