Ebola virus disease – Democratic Republic of the Congo

As of 15 March 2026, a total of 42 confirmed cases including 12 deaths (case fatality ratio 28.6%) have been reported from Ituri Province, Democratic Republic of the Congo.

Cases have been identified across four health zones: Mambasa, Mandima, Nia-Nia and Komanda. Contact tracing is ongoing with 210 contacts currently under surveillance.

WHO is supporting the Ministry of Health with epidemiological investigation, infection prevention and control, and risk communication. Ring vaccination using the rVSV-ZEBOV vaccine has been initiated.

Public health response

The Ministry of Health of the DRC, with support from WHO and partners, has activated the Emergency Operations Centre and deployed a rapid response team to the affected areas.
