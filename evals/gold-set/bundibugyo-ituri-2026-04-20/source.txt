Bundibugyo virus disease - Democratic Republic of the Congo - Disease Outbreak News, 20 April 2026

On 20 April 2026, the Ministry of Health of the DRC confirmed an outbreak of Bundibugyo virus disease in Ituri Province. As of 20 April 2026, 47 confirmed cases and 12 deaths have been reported across three health zones in Ituri.

Rwampara Health Zone reports 28 of the 47 confirmed cases, with 6 distinct health areas affected. Active case finding and contact tracing are ongoing across the zone.

Mongbwalu Health Zone accounts for 16 confirmed cases. Four of the 12 deaths occurred among healthcare workers at Mongbwalu General Referral Hospital, representing a significant nosocomial transmission event. Three patients remain in active treatment at the Ebola Treatment Unit.

Bunia Health Zone has reported 3 confirmed cases. Additionally, laboratory analysis confirms 11 positive results from rapid diagnostic testing conducted across the three zones.

As of this reporting date, cumulative confirmed cases stand at 47, of which 12 have died. Five new cases have been confirmed since the previous situation report issued on 17 April 2026.
