Bundibugyo virus disease - Democratic Republic of the Congo
Disease Outbreak News, 3 June 2026

On 3 June 2026, the World Health Organization was notified of a cluster of Bundibugyo virus disease cases in Ituri Province, Democratic Republic of the Congo. As of 3 June 2026, a total of 18 confirmed cases and 5 deaths have been reported from Mambasa Health Zone.

Laboratory confirmation was obtained by PCR testing at the Institut National de Recherche Biomédicale (INRB) in Kinshasa. Contact tracing has identified 143 contacts under follow-up across three affected health areas.

WHO assesses the risk at the national level as high due to the potential for spread to adjacent health zones. International risk is assessed as low.
