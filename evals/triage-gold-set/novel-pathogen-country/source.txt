Marburg Virus Disease - Rwanda
Disease Outbreak News, 8 June 2026

On 8 June 2026, the Ministry of Health of Rwanda notified the World Health Organization of a confirmed case of Marburg virus disease in Kigali. This is the first confirmed case of Marburg virus disease ever reported in Rwanda.

The case is a healthcare worker at a private clinic in Kigali who developed symptoms on 5 June 2026 and was admitted to an isolation facility on 7 June 2026. Laboratory confirmation was obtained by PCR testing on 8 June 2026.

Contact tracing is ongoing. A total of 52 contacts have been identified and are under monitoring. Epidemiological investigation is underway to determine the source of infection, including potential zoonotic exposure or travel history.

WHO rates the risk at the national level as high given the high case fatality rate of Marburg virus disease and the absence of approved specific treatments or vaccines.
