Ebola virus disease — Democratic Republic of the Congo — 17 July 2019

As of 17 July 2019, the ongoing Ebola virus disease (Zaire) outbreak in the Democratic Republic of the Congo has resulted in 2,512 total cases (2,359 confirmed, 153 probable), including 1,676 deaths (case fatality ratio 66.7%). The outbreak primarily affects North Kivu and Ituri provinces, with active transmission clusters in Beni, Mandima, Mabalako, and Katwa health zones. A total of 131 healthcare workers have been infected, of whom 41 have died. The outbreak was declared a Public Health Emergency of International Concern on 17 July 2019.
